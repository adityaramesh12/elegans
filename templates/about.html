Elegans - a connectome explorer for the nematode worm C. elegans

The adult C. elegans hermaphrodite worm has 302 neurons that belong to two distinct and independent nervous systems: a large somatic nervous system (282 neurons) and a small pharyngeal nervous system (20 neurons). Elegans allows for the exploration of the connectivity between the 279 somatic neurons whose interconnections are best established.

Data sources

The connectome presented here is based mostly on data first discussed by Chen et al. (2006), a full analysis of which can be found in a more recent publication by Varshney et al. (2011). This data is made available on the connectivity pages of the Worm Atlas...

Database

The preprocessed connectome is maintained in Neo4j, a graph database in which nodes and edges are stored along with their meta-information. Neo4j offers a powerful query language (cypher) that is used here to find subgraphs of the full connectome...

Visualization

The network visualization of the connectome is done entirely with d3.js, a client-side javascript framework for producing interactive visualizations in the browser...

References

  2. Varshney, Chen, Paniaqua, Hall and Chklovskii (2011). Structural properties of the C. elegans neuronal network. PLoS Comput. Biol. 3:7:e1001066 (doi:10.1371/journal.pcbi.1001066).
